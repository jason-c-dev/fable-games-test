## 4. Lessons Ledger (violations caused real shipped bugs — reread before P3 and P8)

1. **The verifier's model drifts from the sim.** For every edge type the
   chunk verifier assumes (jump distance at speed, slide length, lane-switch
   time, parry window), write a reality probe that performs it in the real
   sim on real track data. Gen 2 shipped a sealed level and lift-less
   updrafts because the model said "fine" and nothing checked the sim.
2. **No content is exempt from QA.** The Training Grove shipped unplayable
   because gyms were excluded from the verifier. Tutorial, gym, credits
   stretch — all verified.
3. **Flush-contact papercuts:** taps get cut by variable-height logic; bots
   (and players) must HOLD jump; add input latching through hit-stop/slow-mo
   (copy Overgrown's `_latchPressed`); auto-assists beat tuning docs (gen 2's
   auto step-up and no-grab-on-shallow-rims fixed player AND bot).
4. **Attract/demo bots need state handlers for every player state** and
   escalating stuck recovery. The demo is the first gameplay a player
   watches; a stuck bot reads as a broken game.
5. **Test dpr 1 AND 2, multiple aspect ratios,** letterbox/mask correctness.
   `renderer.screen`-style CSS-pixel APIs, never divide by resolution twice.
6. **Playwright key presses need hold time**; item/trigger tests may need to
   snap the camera/wake entities after teleports.
7. **Standalone build**: no top-level await; wrap main in `boot()` from the
   start rather than refactoring later.
8. **Level/chunk authoring in ASCII/JSON by hand WILL have off-by-ones:**
   verify after every authoring batch, use programmatic patchers (don't
   hand-retype rows), and never let a patch overwrite a neighboring feature
   in the same row/chunk (it happened twice).
9. **Sound scheduling**: throttle SFX per name (~45ms), wrap Tone calls in
   try/catch, duck music under big moments.
10. **If a human reports "stuck/unplayable," believe them** — every such
    report in gen 2 was a real bug, and each fix generalized into a probe.
